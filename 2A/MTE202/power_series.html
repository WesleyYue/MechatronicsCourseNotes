Taylor Series

\[\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{f^{(n)}(x_0)}{n!} (x-x_0)^n\]

Steps: Just keep looking for higher \(y^{(n)}(0)\) values and then plug into taylor series

Power Series

\[ \begin{align} f(x) &= \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_n (x-x_0)^n \\ \int f(x) &= \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{a_n}{n+1} (x-x_0)^{n+1}\\ f'(x) &= \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} n a_n (x-x_0)^{n-1}\\ f''(x) &= \sum_{n=2}^{\infty} n(n-1)a_n(x-x_0)^{n-2} \end{align} \]

Radius of convergence \[\lim_{n\to\infty} |\frac{a_n}{a_{n+1}}| = R\]

To use power series on \[p(x)y'' + q(x)y' + r(x)y = 0\] \(x_0\) needs to be an ordinary point, where \(p(x_0) \neq 0\)

Steps:
1. Put \(y\) in terms of power series
2. Massage power series to have the same \(x\) exponents by shifting index
3. Simplify
4. Find equality for every piece of added component
5. Find \(a_n\) for the first few
6. Look for pattern
